# Layout and Density

## Contents

- Density

## Hierarchy

Make the primary task discoverable through order, scale, contrast, grouping, and position. Use alignment to create relationships. Avoid multiple competing headers or primary actions.

## Spacing rhythm

Use a restrained spacing scale, then apply optical correction where type or icons require it. Internal spacing should usually be tighter than separation between groups. Repetition should create rhythm without flattening hierarchy.

## Surface strategy

Choose a small surface vocabulary: flat groups, dividers, tonal regions, borders, or elevation. Cards should represent meaningful grouping, selection, or containment—not every block of content.

## Density

Match frequency and context. Operational tools benefit from compact scanning and predictable alignment; infrequent consumer flows can use more breathing room and explanation. Touch targets remain accessible even when visual density is high.
