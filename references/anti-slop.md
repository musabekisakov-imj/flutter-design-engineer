# Anti-Slop Judgment

## Contents

- Diagnose generic output
- Replace patterns with reasoning
- Preserve useful conventions

## Diagnose generic output

Look for repeated rounded cards, nested tinted surfaces, arbitrary gradients, excessive pills, oversized headings, decorative metrics, uniform spacing, generic icon bubbles, fake charts, and empty marketing language. No single pattern is forbidden; repetition without product meaning is the failure.

## Replace patterns with reasoning

- Replace nested cards with hierarchy, whitespace, dividers, or grouped lists.
- Replace arbitrary accent colors with semantic roles and one controlled emphasis strategy.
- Replace every-control-as-pill with geometry matched to function and platform.
- Replace equal visual weight with explicit primary, secondary, and tertiary actions.
- Replace placeholder copy with realistic content lengths and error conditions.
- Replace ornamental dashboards with the next decision or action the user needs.
- Replace “premium” effects with disciplined type, alignment, rhythm, material, and detail.

Audit whether a flourish improves comprehension, feedback, identity, or delight at acceptable cost. Remove it otherwise.

## Preserve useful conventions

Do not reject Material, Cupertino, cards, gradients, or rounded geometry categorically. Platform conventions reduce learning cost. Distinctiveness should come from coherent composition and product-specific detail, not novelty everywhere.
